## Ready to build (source verified copyleft)

### Math

- **Linear Algebra** — Jim Hefferon, *Linear Algebra* (GFDL + CC BY-SA 2.5). Full textbook with exercises. [hefferon.net](https://hefferon.net/linearalgebra/)
- **Discrete Math** — Oscar Levin, *Discrete Mathematics: An Open Introduction* (CC BY-SA 4.0). Combinatorics, graph theory, logic. [discrete.openmathbooks.org](https://discrete.openmathbooks.org/)
- **Abstract Algebra** — Tom Judson, *Abstract Algebra: Theory and Applications* (GFDL). Groups, rings, monoids. Already on PageLeft. [abstract.ups.edu](http://abstract.ups.edu/)
- **Combinatorics + Optimization** — Keller & Trotter, *Applied Combinatorics* (CC BY-SA). Enumeration, graphs, network flows. [appliedcombinatorics.org](https://www.appliedcombinatorics.org/)

### Computer Science

- **Data Structures** — Pat Morin, *Open Data Structures* (CC BY). Java/C++/Python. [opendatastructures.org](https://opendatastructures.org/) — CC BY (permissive, would need our own CC BY-SA expression)
- **Algorithms** — Jeff Erickson, *Algorithms* (CC BY 4.0). Graduate-level. [jeffe.cs.illinois.edu/teaching/algorithms](https://jeffe.cs.illinois.edu/teaching/algorithms/) — CC BY (same caveat)

### Cognitive Science

- **Foundations of Cognitive Science** — Michael R.W. Dawson, *Mind, Body, World* (CC BY-NC-SA). History, approaches, debates. [open.umn.edu](https://open.umn.edu/opentextbooks/textbooks/170) — NC clause means we can't use commercially. Link only.
- **Computational Cognitive Science** — *Lovelace* textbook (CC BY-SA 4.0). Bayesian models, neural networks, decision making. [computationalcognitivescience.github.io](https://computationalcognitivescience.github.io/lovelace/home)

### Economics / Mechanism Design

- **Algorithmic Game Theory** — Nisan, Roughgarden, Tardos, Vazirani (2007). Published by Cambridge, but the [PDF is freely available](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~sandholm/cs15-892F13/algorithmic-game-theory.pdf). License unclear — needs verification.
- **Mechanism Design** — Wikipedia articles on [mechanism design](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanism_design), [VCG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vickrey%E2%80%93Clarke%E2%80%93Groves_mechanism), [auction theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auction_theory) (all CC BY-SA). Could compile into a coherent sequence.

### Design & Architecture

- **Design Patterns** — Wikibooks, *Introduction to Software Engineering/Design Patterns* (CC BY-SA). Thin but extensible. [en.wikibooks.org](https://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Introduction_to_Software_Engineering/Architecture/Design_Patterns)
- **Architecture of Open Source Applications** — Amy Brown & Greg Wilson, eds. (CC BY 3.0). 25 open source apps explained. [aosabook.org](https://aosabook.org/) — CC BY (permissive)

## Wikipedia as source

Wikipedia is CC BY-SA 4.0. Any article can be extended into a reading page. High-value targets (ideas from the /books/ list that have good Wikipedia coverage):

- [System 1 and System 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_process_theory) (Kahneman)
- [Pattern language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pattern_language) (Alexander)
- [Society of mind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society_of_Mind) (Minsky)
- [Lean manufacturing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lean_manufacturing) (Toyota Way)
- [Parkinson's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parkinson%27s_law) (Mythical Man-Month)
- [Broken windows theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broken_windows_theory) (Clean Code / Pragmatic Programmer)
- [Prospect theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prospect_theory) (Kahneman)
- [Confirmation bias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confirmation_bias) (Thinking Fast and Slow)
- [Conway's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conway%27s_law) (Organizational Patterns)
- [Brook's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brooks%27s_law) (Mythical Man-Month)

These bridge the /books/ shelf to the /reading/ shelf. The book is the idea's origin. The Wikipedia article is the CC BY-SA expression. The reading page is the runnable derivative.

## Not planned (wrong license)

- Most O'Reilly books (all rights reserved)
- Most academic textbooks (all rights reserved)
- Anything NC (non-commercial clause blocks Canon's growth)
- Anything ND (no derivatives — can't extend)
